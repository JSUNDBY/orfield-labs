01 — About

An independent, multi-sensory
design research laboratory

Orfield Laboratories is a perceptually-based research and consulting laboratory serving North American and international clients in human comfort research, architecture, design services, and product development testing.

Operating since 1971, the lab houses the Guinness World Record-holding anechoic chamber — the quietest place on earth at -24.9 dBA. The facility spans 15,000 square feet of multi-sensory testing environments in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

The lab is a Federal, NVLAP NIST-Accredited Acoustic Lab (Lab Code 200248), providing objective and subjective measurement services across acoustics, lighting, thermal comfort, and indoor air quality.

08 — History

A History of Listening

From a basement practice to the quietest place on earth. From the first digital recording studio to a world-class research laboratory.

Steven J. Orfield

In 1966, a young philosophy student at the University of Minnesota began studying the philosophy of science. He would never finish his degree, but the discipline shaped everything that followed.

Steven Orfield founded the lab in January 1971, operating from his basement with a radical premise: that human perception should drive design. Within a decade it became the first independent multi-sensory building performance consulting laboratory in the United States.

"I learned that a question doesn't imply an answer. It implies a suspicious-looking question. It isn't solving problems that we need skill at. It's defining them, because once you define them correctly, solutions jump at you."

Emma Orfield Johnston

A Parsons School of Design graduate with a background in integrated design and psychology, Emma is the only family member to ever work alongside her grandfather. She started interning at the lab at age fifteen.

After returning to Minneapolis full-time in 2021, Emma has expanded the lab's reach into therapeutic and wellness experiences, developing silent retreat sessions in the anechoic chamber and leading group tours that connect visitors with the profound experience of absolute quiet.

Together, Steven and Emma represent two generations of the same conviction: that human perception is the most important instrument in the room.

Sound 80 Studios

The building at 2709 East 25th Street was constructed in 1970 as Sound 80 Studios. What happened inside rewrote music history.

Bob Dylan — Blood on the Tracks

December 1974. Dylan re-recorded five tracks for what many consider his greatest album, working with local musicians Peter Ostroushko, Billy Peterson, and Bill Berg.

Prince — The Beginning

Late 1976. A teenage Prince began recording demos across the hall from Cat Stevens, singing every part and playing every instrument himself. Those demos led to his Warner Bros. contract.

Lipps, Inc. — "Funkytown"

Recorded in Studio 2. Four weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100. Chart-topping in 28 countries. The first number-one single from Minnesota.

The First Digital Recording

In 1978, 3M chose Sound 80 to test the world's first commercial digital recording system. The Saint Paul Chamber Orchestra's recording became the first digital audio to win a Grammy.

Orfield purchased the building in 1990. In 2020, Sound 80 was added to the National Register of Historic Places.

Studio 2 — where Funkytown was recorded

The Quietest Place on Earth

A six-sided steel double-walled box, suspended on vibration-absorbing springs inside a five-sided steel outer chamber. Those two rooms sit inside a laboratory with 12-inch concrete walls. Glass-fibre wedges absorb 99.99% of sound.

Inside, visitors stand on a suspended mesh platform. With the lights off, there is nothing. People report hearing their own heartbeat, blood moving through their veins, their eyelids blinking. After thirty minutes, many need to sit down as balance deteriorates without ambient sound.

The chamber was originally built by Sunbeam for product testing in Chicago. Orfield purchased it in the 1980s and installed it in 1994.
