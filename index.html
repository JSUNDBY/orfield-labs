01 — About

An independent, multi-sensory
design research laboratory

Orfield Laboratories is a perceptually-based research and consulting laboratory serving North American and international clients in human comfort research, architecture, design services, and product development testing.

Operating since 1971, the lab houses the Guinness World Record-holding anechoic chamber — the quietest place on earth at -24.9 dBA. The facility spans 15,000 square feet of multi-sensory testing environments in Minneapolis, Minnesota.

The lab is a Federal, NVLAP NIST-Accredited Acoustic Lab (Lab Code 200248), providing objective and subjective measurement services across acoustics, lighting, thermal comfort, and indoor air quality.

02 — Services

Testing & Product Development

Full perceptually-based testing in acoustics, audio-visual systems, lighting, daylighting, vision, thermal comfort, and indoor air quality. Comfort, performance, and preference — measured.

Acoustic Testing

Sound intensity, acoustic holography, and sound quality measurement. NVLAP NIST-accredited since the 1970s. Home of the Sound Quality Working Group (SQWG).

Lighting & Vision

Visual performance testing, daylighting analysis, and lighting simulation for optimal human perception and cognitive comfort in built environments.

Thermal & Air Quality

Indoor air quality assessment and thermal comfort evaluation using perceptual and cognitive comfort standards for building performance.

Product Development

Design, research, and testing for appliances, vehicles, audio-visual systems, medical devices, aircraft, tools, and consumer electronics. Human perception at the core.
